Declaration of Independence


Americans were no longer under British rule. Instead, the thirteen British colonies came together to become a union of free and independent states.


In the Declaration, Thomas Jefferson starts by writing about people's rights, and what the government should and should not do.


He then lists specific bad things that King George III did to the American colonies. The king put people in jail with no reason, made taxes that were too high, and did not respect people who lived in the colonies.
