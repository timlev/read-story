Space Clothes

What do astronauts wear in space, where the environment is so different from Earth? In space, there is no oxygen and no air pressure. Also, space is either extremely hot or extremely cold. The clothes astronauts wear must protect them from these dangerous conditions.

Inside and Outside the Shuttle

Inside the space shuttle, astronauts can wear ordinary, comfortable clothes. Outside the shuttle, however, they must wear special suits called EMUs. EMU stands for Extravehicular Mobility Unit. EMUs protect astronauts while they do their jobs outside the shuttle.

Putting on the Suit

An EMU has many parts. First, an astronaut puts on a full body suit that keeps him or her cool. Then, the astronaut puts on a protective top and bottom. The last things the astronaut puts on are the gloves and helmet. Now it's time to put on the most important piece the backpack!

Space Survival

The backpack contains the Primary Life Support System, which helps astronauts breathe in space. It also allows them to communicate with the other astronauts. Without their special clothing and equipment astronauts could not survive or do their job.
