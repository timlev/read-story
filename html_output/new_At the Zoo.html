At the Zoo

Leda and Tess are best friends.

They are at the zoo.

They like to look at the animals.

They look at the lions.

They look at the tigers.

They look at the elephants.

They look at the giraffes.

Then Leda and Tess look at the brown bears.

They read the sign about the brown bears.

Brown bears are always looking for food.

They can weight 1,000 pounds.

They have long fur.

They have sharp teeth and claws.

They sleep in the winter.

They like to eat fish.

Leda laughs.

"This animal is like my husband," she says.

"That isn't true," answers Tess.

"Your husband doesn't like fish."
